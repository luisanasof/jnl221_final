When PEN America first offered her job in tracking cases of banned books across the country, Sabrina Baeta only expected to work for the organization’s pilot research project for three months. At the time, the study was an “informal response” to an observed movement happening in the country, Baeta said.

That was in 2021. Today, Baeta continues to co-manage the organization’s databases of book bans or book ban attempts, which together list over 4,000 such challenges in school districts across the United States in the last academic year and a half.
